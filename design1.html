DigiJudo

Mobile Application Prototype

Project Overview

This project is done in partial fulfillment of the requirements for the module CSD3125.

DigiJudo is a mobile application designed and developed for mobile smart devices such as phones and tablets to help facilitate various operational, administrative and management matters of the DigiPen Judo Club.

Requirements

The DigiJudo application must have the following features:

  • Create a new account and sign in
  • Show current events and schedule
  • View available training slots
  • Book group or private training sessions and able to cancel sessions
  • Sign up for a belt test - a Master needs to approve the sign-ups
  • Buy equipment such as uniforms and sparring gear
  • Little Dragon Access (Helicopter mode) - the parents decide what the kids are allowed to do and what not, namely which areas of the application the kids can access and which areas they cannot access.

USER ANALYSIS

User Class

There are several users identified: students (Dragons), staff & faculty (Gray Dragons), and children of staff and faculty members (Little Dragons).

Based on the target users from the assignment brief, we have grouped the users into:

  • Trainees - Student, Untrained Staff & Faculty
  • Trainers - Trained Staff & Faculty
  • Kids - Children of Staff & Faculty

User Persona

Peter

  • Student at SIT
  • Has some experience in Judo. Ranked as White Belt - 5th kyu
  • Challenges: Lack of time, WhatsApp has too many messages, the important messages are buried
  • Goals: To be able to check training schedule, book training sessions, check for important events, and sign up

Jake - John's son

  • Primary school student
  • No experience in Judo
  • Not well-versed in English, may not be able to use mobile devices well
  • Goals: Learn basic judo

LO-FI PROTOTYPE

For the low-fidelity prototypes, I drew the screens on tablet and printed it out for paper prototyping testing. During the low-fidelity prototype development, after each round of feedback from the testers, the prototype was improved based on feedback and the new version was tested with a different tester.

First Paper Prototype

Evaluation of First Paper Prototype

Feedback:

  • Schedule and 'Event' buttons in the home page are confusing as there is also a 'Schedule' tab at the bottom of the screen.
  • There shouldn't be a 'Shop' button in the home menu if there is parental control.
  • Event functions is unclear on what exactly it does.

Conclusion:

  • Rename 'Schedule' button in the navigation bar to 'Calendar'.
  • Include error messages if a child tries to access the shop page, such as "Access Denied", so that the little dragon will be unable to purchase items themselves.

Second Paper Prototype

Evaluation of Second Paper Prototype

Feedback:

  • Schedule and 'Event' buttons in the home page are confusing as there is also a 'Schedule' tab at the bottom of the screen.
  • Some buttons in the home page are unclear or somewhat do the same thing in the end, and users are not very confident in what they are pressing.
  • Redundant to have a "Book Session" button in home page as users can do so by pressing the "Book" button in the navigation bar.
  • "Cancel Session" and "Remove" are inconsistent.
  • No checkout page to test when purchasing an item from the shop.
  • No feedback for confirmation after an action.
  • Unable to select the number of people attending the group session.
  • Unable to see how many slots are left for the session.

Conclusion:

  • Remove redundant buttons on the home page that perform similar functions to reduce confusion.
  • Remove "Book" button from navigation bar as users can book the session from the "Training Schedule" page.
  • Add confirmation screens for every decision made, such as when cancelling a booking.
  • Events can only be cancelled from the calendar.
  • Rename "Add Schedule" to "Add Slot" to better represent that the master is adding a slot to the training schedule.
  • Remove "Manage" button in navigation bar and its associated page.
  • "Belt Test Sign Up" page for masters will be renamed to "Belt Tests". The page will also show all the applications at once to make it easier to view the application status and to approve or reject it.

HI-FI PROTOTYPE

Final Prototype

The final prototype was created in Figma based on all the feedbacks received from the low-fidelity prototype tests.

DigiJudo Logo

For this project, to keep it simple, we decided to go with a minimalist and monochrome theme. As the application is for a judo club, I imagined the letter 'O' to be a sumo. Hence, I added a judo belt on the letter to make it seem like a sumo wrestler. The belt was drawn out using Figma as well.

Master PIN

Masters will need a unique verification PIN that identifies them as a registered coach by the school to sign up for a new account. This PIN will be given to them by the school as a unique identification number. This ensures that only SIT Staff can create a Master account.

Little Dragon Access

When accessing the Little Dragon Access, users with child accounts linked to their email will have a list of Little Dragons (accounts of children registered and linked to their email), and they can choose individual access permissions for each account.

View Events

Events tab is removed from the quick access panel on the home page and a "View Events" link is added next to "Upcoming Events", which will scroll through a few recent events. When the “View Events” link is tapped, the user will be brought to the Events page, where they can see all past and upcoming events.
